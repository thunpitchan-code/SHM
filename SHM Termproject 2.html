🎯 Introduction · Preview

Introduction to Structural Health Monitoring

Background

Civil infrastructure such as bridges, buildings, and dams are subject to gradual deterioration over time due to environmental loading, material aging, and unexpected events such as earthquakes or heavy traffic. Traditional inspection methods rely on visual checks and periodic manual assessments, which are often costly, time-consuming, and insufficient to detect early-stage damage.

Problem Statement

Without a continuous and reliable monitoring system, structural damage may go undetected until it reaches a critical stage — posing serious safety risks and increasing repair costs significantly. There is a growing need for an automated, real-time approach to assess the health of civil structures.

Objectives

1. To study the principles and technologies of Structural Health Monitoring (SHM) 2. To review existing SHM systems applied in real construction projects 3. To analyze sensor data and evaluate damage detection performance 4. To recommend suitable SHM strategies for civil structures in Thailand

Scope of Study

This study focuses on SHM systems applied to civil engineering structures, particularly bridges and multi-story buildings. The research covers sensor technologies, data acquisition systems, signal processing methods, and real-world case studies. The study is limited to non-destructive monitoring approaches and does not include structural repair or retrofitting methods.

Significance of Study

Implementing SHM can extend the service life of structures, reduce maintenance costs, and improve public safety. This research contributes to the advancement of smart infrastructure practices in civil engineering.

📊 Results & Analysis · SHM Overview

Structural Health Monitoring — Key Findings

What is SHM?

Structural Health Monitoring (SHM) is a process of implementing a damage detection and characterization strategy for engineering structures. It involves continuous or periodic monitoring of a structure's condition using embedded or attached sensors to detect damage, deterioration, or changes in structural behavior over time.

Key Sensor Types Used

🔊 Accelerometers ⚡ Strain Gauges 🌡️ Temperature Sensors 🔵 Piezoelectric Sensors 📡 Fiber Optic (FBG) 📷 Acoustic Emission

Analysis Methods

Data collected from sensors is processed using signal processing techniques such as Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) and wavelet analysis to identify changes in natural frequencies, mode shapes, and damping ratios — all indicators of structural damage. Machine learning algorithms are increasingly used to improve damage classification accuracy.

Typical Applications

🌉 Bridges 🏢 High-rise Buildings 🏭 Industrial Structures ✈️ Aerospace 🌊 Offshore Platforms 🏗️ Dams
